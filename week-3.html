  • Traditionally the author of the Pentateuch has been identified as Moses

Authorship Issues

  • German higher-criticism 1800's
  • Wellhausen proposes the Documentary Hypothesis (JEDP)

The Documentary Hypothesis

  • Each source is a complete, independent account
  • Redaction criticism is the art of trying to determine where the seams between sources are
  • Yahwist Source (J) - Shares the bulk of the authorship of Genesis with E
  • Elohist Source (E) - Shares the bulk of the authorship of Genesis with J
  • Deuteronomist Source (D) - Source from the time of Josiah involved in the renewal of monotheistic Yahwism
  • Priestly Source (P) - The final source of the Hebrew Bible, sometimes including the redactor (R) or identified as the redactors themselves which represent a distinct view of the priesthood

The Documentary Hypothesis Today

  • J & E are rarely thought of as separate sources from one another
  • Late 19th century, German, anti-catholicism has been said to have read into both the motives and timing of P
  • A more favorable theory recently published (2003) suggests a diminished J-E separation with P and D flipped

Supplementary Hypothesis

  • A variation on Documentary Hypothesis that sees development of the text over time, but development into a single source tradition (D)
  • This view tends to be more comfortably held by conservative scholars who are not onboard with a full mosaic authorship
  • This is closer to the model I hold, as I presented in the chart last week.

Covenants

The Torah covenants are effectively Suzerainty Treaties

  • Defines role of sovereign
  • Defines role of the vassal
  • Defines how order is to be maintained in the relationship
  • Breaking covenant is described as rebellion rather than apostasy or breaking

YHWH places his Name in Israel

  • YHWH extends his identity through his covenant with Israel
  • Israel's actions either elevate or impinge on the reputation of YHWH

In a covenant relationship, the covenant continues as long as the initiator wills it.

Literary Structure

The Pentateuch is characterized by both an easily discernible unity and a noticeable lack of uniformity. The history of the study of the book is often marked by the tendency to stress one of these characteristics over against the other. [...] Critical scholarship [sees] the lack of uniformity of style and vocabulary as a sign of the lack of unity in the structure and message of the book. [...] Conservatives have often ignored the rough edges in the narratives, thinking that in so doing they were safeguarding the unity of the book.

Sailhamer, J. H. 1992. The Pentateuch as Narrative: A Biblical-Theological Commentary. Zondervan Publishing House.
