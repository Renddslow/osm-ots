Human & Divine

While limited, a helpful metaphor for understanding this unified project may be found in the confession of the Church that Christ is both God and Man. We neither can confound his nature nor divide his personhood. Similarly, the Church confesses Christ as having two wills, one Divine and one human, yet both operated in full cooperation with one another, never being coerced or held hostage by one or the other. In a similar fashion, we may understand the cooperation of the Spirit and the human authors.

McElwee, M., 2021. Position Paper on Hermeneutics. [online] MattMcElwee.com. Available at: <https://bit.ly/3OXl7AQ> [Accessed 3 May 2022].

Scope of Inspiration

  • Inspiration as we're describing it is within the context of the closed-canon of Scripture
  • The Holy Spirit didn't normatively dictate the words of Scripture
  • The human writers were not normatively in an ecstatic state when writing
  • The human writers were not themselves inspired
  • The whole canon is inspired

Unified

The Bible has many authors, literary styles, and themes, but it tells one story about God's plan to rescue humanity and make them his partners in ruling the world.

Mackie, T., host. "The Bible Had Editors." BibleProject Podcast [podcast audio].
27 September 2021. Available at: <https://bibleproject.com/podcast/bible-had-editors/> [Accessed 3 May 2022].

  • Worth mentioning the deuterocanon
  • Unified is the basis for much Biblical theology
  • Genesis as the starting point for the unified story

Messianic

Meditation

  • Psalm 1 and Deuteronomy 6
  • Scripture was not meant for a single reading but for a life-time (Revelation 12)
  • The Hebrew word for meditate (hagah) literally means to mutter ‚Äì it's the sound of chewing

Wisdom

  • The Bible presents two ways of thinking about wisdom:
    1. Wisdom as defined by YHWH
    2. Wisdom as defined by untrusting, scarcity-minded humans
  • The Hebrew word for wisdom (chokmah) can also mean skill or craft
  • The wisdom of Scripture is a gift
  • Do techne + Bezalel
  • Black & white commands (murder, honoring father and mother)

Contextually-Rooted

  • The Hebrew Bible is written over the course of somewhere between 900 and 1200 years
  • The Hebrew Bible is written by people who start to finish would've had profound cultural distance
  • The Hebrew Bible is not written in English ‚Äì it's written in Hebrew
  • Vastly different cultures
    • Race/Ethnicity
    • Purity
    • Patriarchal
    • Honor/Shame
    • Collectivist
    • Slave-Ownership
