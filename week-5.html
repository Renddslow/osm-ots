Style

Style

Styles in the Bible

  • Narrative: A text that makes its point through story telling (e.g. Genesis, Judges, Samuel)
  • Prose-Discourse: A text that presents an idea or series of ideas (e.g. covenant obligations from the Torah, Pauline epistles)
  • Poetry: A text where normal language is modified and intensified (e.g. Psalms)

Styles in the Bible

  • Prose-Discourse operates on the basis of logic and exposing fallacies. It might be eloquent or have rhetorical flourish, but ultimately it can be boiled down to logical argumentation.
  • Narrative can be summarized by a synopsis.
  • Poetry can neither be boiled down or summarized.

Hebrew Poetry & Psalms

Distinguishing elements of Hebrew Poetry

  • Does not tend to use meter or rhyme
  • Concentrated use of imagery and figures of speech
  • Parallelism

Parallelism

Parallelism is the regularly recurring juxtaposition of symmetrical units (cola) so that there is a relationship of correspondence between them

Hebrew poetry rhymes ideas instead of sounds

Types of Parallelism

Echoing, a.k.a, "semantic equivalence," "semantic correspondence," "synonymous parallelism"

The second unit expresses roughly the same idea as the first with no additional meaning (if not less)

YHWH is my light and my salvation
YHWH is the stronghold of my life
whom
of whom
shall I fear
shall I be afraid

Psalm 27:1

Types of Parallelism

Progressive, a.k.a, "extending," "synthetic parallelism"

The second unit relates to the idea of the first, but adds another thought element to it

Vindicate me
For I have led
o YHWH
a blameless life

Psalm 26:1

You broaden the path beneath me
so that my ankles do not turn

Psalm 18:36

Types of Parallelism

Inverted, a.k.a, "mirror parallelism"

The second unit expresses essentially the same idea as the first, but the order is reversed

O YHWH
be not far from me,
do not forsake me;
O my God

Psalm 38:21

Types of Parallelism

Antithetic parallelism

The second unit expresses the opposite side of the same truth, but usually with the adversative "but"


BUT
I
I
hate and abhor
love
falsehood
your torah

Psalm 119:163

Other Literary Features in Hebrew Poetry

  • Acrostic An alphabet poem (Ps. 119, Pss. 9-10)
  • Inclusio The opening and closing words of a poem when they are identical (Ps. 103, Ps. 118)
  • Chiasm Lines parallel one another in a mirror format (Ps. 67, Ps. 70)

Wisdom Literature

  • Lower Wisdom - Prudential, Pragmatic, the kind your father would give you around the dinner table
  • Higher Wisdom - Philosophical, Cynical/Critical, Speculative

Purpose of Wisdom

  1. Provide a godly grid for interpreting life
  2. To teach general principles about the way life usually works
  3. Provide training in areas of living

A mashal is a wise saying, proverb, or parallel and is the most common way of describing the types of lower wisdom sayings found in Proverbs

Wisdom Books and their types

  • Proverbs - Lower Wisdom
  • Ecclesiastes - Higher Wisdom
  • Job - Higher Wisdom
  • Song of Songs - Lower Wisdom

Exile Literature

  • Some of the latest literature in Scripture
  • Primarily shaped by the experience of the people post-Exile
    • Second Temple is built
    • Messianic-like figures come and go (Zerubbabel and Joshua)
    • Renewal movements rise and fall
    • The people experience a series of what seems to be exiles within the land they are living

The Shape of the Exile Literature

  1. Chronicles (How the exile came to be)
  2. Lamentations (Jerusalem has fallen)
  3. Daniel (How to live as faithful, covenant people in Exile)
  4. Ezra-Nehemiah (The disappointment of the Second Temple)
  5. Ruth (A response to Ezra: YHWH's blessing extends to all nations who commit to covenant hesed)
  6. Esther (How to live as a faithful, covenant people in Diaspora)
  7. 2 Chronicles 36:22-23 (There is still hope)
